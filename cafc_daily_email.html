WRIGHT v. COLLINSPRECEDENTIAL
Appeal No. 25-1502 | Origin: CAVC | OPINION
This case involved a veteran's petition for extraordinary relief seeking contempt sanctions against the Board of Veterans' Appeals Chair and direct grant of benefits claims that had been repeatedly remanded for further development. The Federal Circuit dismissed for lack of jurisdiction, affirming that the Veterans Court cannot review Board remand orders (which are non-final) and has no authority under the All Writs Act to grant substantive relief without remand, citing *Ravin v. McDonough* and *Gardner-Dickson v. Wilkie*. This decision reinforces that veterans cannot circumvent jurisdictional limitations on appealing Board remand orders by seeking mandamus relief, limiting strategic options for addressing prolonged claim adjudication delays.

In Re MILLER MENDEL, INC.
Appeal No. 25-147 | Origin: RIT | ORDER
**In Re Miller Mendel, Inc.** - The Federal Circuit denied a mandamus petition seeking to compel transfer of a patent case from the Eastern District of California to the Western District of Oklahoma, where related litigation was already pending. The court found no clear abuse of discretion in the district court's denial of transfer, particularly given that the petitioner waited until after receiving an unfavorable motion to dismiss ruling before seeking transfer, and that the California action involved additional claims beyond those in the Oklahoma case. This decision reinforces that mandamus relief for transfer denials requires extraordinary circumstances and that strategic timing of transfer motions may weigh against relief.
In Re LOPEZ
Appeal No. 25-151 | Origin: RIT | ORDER
**In Re Lopez (Fed. Cir. 2025)** - The Federal Circuit denied a mandamus petition challenging the Court of Federal Claims' denial of in forma pauperis status, finding that because IFP denials are immediately appealable, mandamus relief was unavailable. The court converted the mandamus petition into a regular appeal but stayed proceedings pending consideration of whether the appeal should be dismissed as frivolous given petitioner's history of filing frivolous complaints. Patent practitioners should note this reinforces that mandamus is not available when adequate appellate remedies exist, and courts will scrutinize appeals from litigants with a pattern of frivolous filings.
NWOSU v. HEWLETT PACKARD ENTERPRISE CO.
Appeal No. 25-2035 | Origin: DCT | ORDER
**NWOSU v. HEWLETT PACKARD ENTERPRISE CO. (Fed. Cir. 2025)** The Federal Circuit dismissed an appeal for lack of jurisdiction where the plaintiff attempted to appeal a district court's striking of a frivolous venue transfer notice in a case involving fraud, antitrust, and unfair business practices claims—but no patent issues. The court reaffirmed that it only has jurisdiction over district court cases involving patent laws, appeals from USPTO proceedings, and certain small damages claims against the United States under 28 U.S.C. § 1295. This serves as a reminder to practitioners that the Federal Circuit's jurisdiction is limited and appellants cannot circumvent regional circuit jurisdiction by filing in the Federal Circuit when no patent law issues are present.
